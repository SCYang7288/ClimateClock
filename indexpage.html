Climate Time 1.5°C: Refers to the goal of keeping the global average temperature rise to no more than 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels (1850–1900). This is a key commitment of the Paris Agreement, aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions and avoiding the most severe impacts of climate change. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) warns that exceeding 1.5°C will greatly increase the risks of extreme weather, sea level rise, and ecosystem collapse. The global temperature has already risen by about 1.1°C, and if emissions are not reduced, it is expected to exceed this threshold between 2030 and 2035.
